# How to Use This AI-Native Textbook

Welcome to an innovative learning experience! ## 1. Navigating the Content

The book is structured into seven main parts, each comprising several chapters and lessons. Use the **sidebar navigation** on the left to browse through topics sequentially or jump directly to areas of interest. Each chapter and lesson is designed to build upon previous knowledge, so a sequential approach is recommended for beginners.

## 2. Interactive "Ask AI" Features

Throughout the textbook, you will find integrated AI capabilities designed to enhance your learning:

*   **Ask AI**: At any point, you can click the "Ask AI" button to pose questions related to the textbook's content. This feature leverages a Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) system to provide context-aware answers derived directly from the book, helping to clarify concepts, elaborate on topics, or cross-reference information.
*   **Ask from Selected Text Only**: Highlight a specific passage and click "Ask from Selected Text Only." The AI will then answer your question based *only* on the selected text, allowing for highly focused inquiry and deeper comprehension of particular sections.
*   **Personalize Content**: This button allows you to tailor the presentation of content to your learning style. Whether you prefer more detailed explanations, simplified analogies, or different examples, the AI can adapt the text to suit your needs. (Note: This feature may require initial setup and preferences.)

## 3. Multilingual Support

Understanding that knowledge transcends linguistic barriers, this textbook offers translation capabilities:

*   **Translate to Urdu**: Click this button to instantly translate the current chapter or lesson into Urdu. This feature aims to make complex technical concepts accessible to a broader audience.

## 4. Code Examples and Simulations

Physical AI is best learned by doing. This textbook includes:

*   **Python + ROS 2 Example Code**: Practical code snippets are integrated directly into lessons, demonstrating concepts discussed. You are encouraged to run and experiment with these examples.
*   **Isaac Sim and Gazebo Configuration Examples**: Detailed configurations for popular simulation environments are provided, enabling you to build and test virtual robotic systems.

## 5. Active Learning and Engagement

We encourage you to:
*   **Experiment**: Modify code, change simulation parameters, and observe the results.
*   **Discuss**: Engage with peers and instructors using the integrated discussion features (if available in your deployment).
*   **Question**: Don't hesitate to use the "Ask AI" features for clarification.

By actively engaging with the material and utilizing the interactive tools provided, you will gain a profound understanding of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics, preparing you for a rewarding career in this frontier field. Happy learning!
